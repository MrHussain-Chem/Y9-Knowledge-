Atomic Structure & Periodic Table

Atomic Structure

  • Atom: Nucleus (protons + neutrons) surrounded by electrons in shells
  • Mass number = protons + neutrons (top number)
  • Atomic number = number of protons (bottom number)
  • Relative masses: Proton = 1, Neutron = 1, Electron = 1/1836
  • Electronic structure: 1st shell max 2e⁻, 2nd & 3rd shell max 8e⁻

The Periodic Table

Modern Periodic Table

  • Groups (columns): Same outer electrons
  • Periods (rows): Same electron shells
  • Arranged by atomic number
  • Same group = similar properties

Historical Development

  • Newlands: Arranged by atomic weight, no gaps, no Group 0
  • Mendeleev: Arranged by atomic weight, left gaps for unknown elements

Group 1 - Alkali Metals

  • Reactivity increases down group
  • React with water to form metal hydroxide + hydrogen
  • React with oxygen to form metal oxide
  • Why reactivity increases: More electron shells → outer electron further from nucleus → easier to lose outer electron

Group 7 - Halogens

  • Reactivity decreases down group
  • More reactive displaces less reactive
  • Why reactivity decreases: More electron shells → outer shell further from nucleus → harder to attract electrons

Key Reactions

  • Metal + Water → Metal hydroxide + H₂
  • Metal + O₂ → Metal oxide

Quantitative Chemistry

Relative Formula Mass (Mr)

  • Step 1: Identify elements
  • Step 2: Count atoms of each
  • Step 3: Find Ar from periodic table
  • Step 4: Multiply atoms × Ar
  • Step 5: Add all up

Example: CaCO₃ = 40 + 12 + (16 × 3) = 100

Percentage Mass

% of element = (Ar of element × atoms of element ÷ Mr of compound) × 100

Example: % O in CaCO₃ = (48 ÷ 100) × 100 = 48%

Balanced Equations

  • Same atoms both sides
  • Only change big numbers
  • Example: 2Mg + O₂ → 2MgO

Using Resources - Earth's Resources

Natural Resources

  • Finite: Cannot be replaced (fossil fuels, metals)
  • Renewable: Replaced within lifetime (wood, water)

Water Treatment

  • Potable water: Safe to drink (low salt, low microbes)
  • Freshwater treatment:
    • Collection → Storage → Screening
    • Coagulation → Sedimentation → Filtration
    • Sterilisation → Neutralisation
  • Desalination: Remove salt (distillation/reverse osmosis)
  • Sewage treatment:
    • Screening → Sedimentation
    • Aerobic digestion of effluent → Sterilisation
    • Anaerobic digestion of sludge

Tests

  • Sodium ions: Flame test (yellow)
  • Chloride ions: HNO₃ + AgNO₃ (white precipitate)
  • Pure water: Boils at exactly 100°C

Life Cycle Assessment

  • Assess environmental impact
  • Stages: Extraction → Manufacture → Use → Disposal

Earth's Atmosphere

Evolution of Atmosphere

  • Phase 1: Volcanic activity (CO₂, H₂O, N₂)
  • Phase 2: Water vapour condensed → oceans
  • Phase 3: Photosynthesis increased O₂, decreased CO₂

Current Composition

  • Nitrogen: ~78%
  • Oxygen: ~21%
  • Argon: ~1%
  • Carbon dioxide: ~0.04%

Greenhouse Effect

  • Greenhouse gases: CO₂, CH₄, H₂O vapour
  • Absorb and re-emit thermal radiation
  • Keep Earth warm for life
  • Enhanced effect: Human activities increase gases

Climate Change

  • Causes: Fossil fuels, deforestation, agriculture
  • Effects: Sea level rise, extreme weather
  • Solutions: Renewable energy, carbon capture

Carbon Footprints

  • Total greenhouse gas emissions
  • Reduce by: Renewables, efficient transport

Key Calculations & Equations for Higher Tier

Relative Formula Mass

Mr = Add up all Ar values

Example: H₂SO₄ = (1×2) + 32 + (16×4) = 98

Percentage Mass

% of element = (Ar of element × atoms of element ÷ Mr of compound) × 100

Example: % O in H₂O = (16 ÷ 18) × 100 = 89%

Rf Value

Rf = distance moved by colour (solute) ÷ distance moved by solvent

Example: Colour moves 4cm, solvent moves 10cm: Rf = 4 ÷ 10 = 0.4
